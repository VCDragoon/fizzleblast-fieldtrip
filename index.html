Session Recap

The Story So Far

The Charisma Company — Moor, Opal, Aramil, Lavender, and Bob — were hired by the neurotic gnome Fizzleblast to retrieve a Golden Snake Idol from a temple deep in the Chultian Jungle. They arrived at noon, dealt with Girallons at the entrance by melting one with a redirected mirror beam and intimidating the other. Inside, they navigated a deadly antechamber of pressure plates by performing a synchronized "Man and Crocodile" walk and piling gold on the traps.

In the Prism Room, Opal was blinded by a solar beam after rolling a Nat 1, poison gas nearly killed the entire party before Lavender's Gust of Wind cleared it and Moor's divine power obliterated seven skeletons in a single moment. A Giant Constrictor Snake crushed Aramil to within an inch of his life before Opal — still blind — delivered a devastating 30-damage critical hit to decapitate it. "I just listened for the screaming," she said later. "Aramil screams a lot."

Bob performed the heist of the century: an Indiana Jones-style oil-for-scales swap followed by a Batman-like flight using the Cloak of the Bat to steal the Golden Snake Idol from above a sleeping T-Rex. Rival mercenaries cast Fireball, which Bob dodged with a natural 20, waking the dinosaur. The party barely escaped through a timed skill challenge to Fizzleblast's magical safety bubble.

In the final twist, Aramil secretly pocketed the Eye of the Serpent from the idol — plunging the entire continent of Faerun into eternal darkness. "Correlation does not imply causation," he muttered. "That is basic science."

Combat Play-by-Play

Turn-by-turn breakdowns of every encounter, narrated in sports-commentary style
01
Gorallon Ambush
1 Round • 6 Turns • Party Damage: 9 dealt, 0 taken
▼

Round 1 kicks off before anyone can even roll initiative! Moor sees the thermal mirrors and has an idea — he angles the massive reflective array directly at Gorallon Number One. The beam hits, the ape tries to dodge, walks into ANOTHER beam, and it's cooked steak and burnt hair! One Gorallon down without a single weapon swing!

Lavender follows with Eldritch Blast — 9 force damage rocks Gorallon 2 backward! Aramil steps up with Speak with Animals and an Intimidation roll. First attempt: Natural 1. But with advantage, he rolls a 22 — the Gorallon freezes, whimpers, and flees into the jungle.

Total combat time: approximately 90 seconds. Efficiency rating: flawless.

02
Giant Constrictor Snake
3 Rounds • 19 Turns • Party Damage: 29 dealt, 22 taken • 1 DOWNED
▼

Round 1: The snake emerges from the statue's mouth. Aramil opens with Eldritch Blast — misses at 9. Opal swings blind with her Great Axe — 12, miss! Aramil gets a second shot — 6 force damage! Tika the raven swoops in and gouges out an eye for 7 piercing! Moor hits with Toll the Dead — 8 necrotic. Bob sneaks in for 8 piercing with Sneak Attack (rolling 1 on his d6 — classic Bob dice).

Round 2: The snake retaliates! Bites paralyzed Tika for 5 damage, then turns on Opal with a constrict — but she flexes out with a 24 Strength save! Bob lands another Sneak Attack for 8 more (rolling 1,2 on damage dice — the dice HATE him).

Round 3: DISASTER. The snake auto-crits paralyzed Tika — familiar destroyed. It grapples Aramil (Athletics 5 vs. 18 — not even close) and SQUEEZES for 22 damage. Aramil drops to -10 HP! The table holds its breath...

Then Opal — BLIND Opal — swings her Great Axe. NATURAL 20! 30 DAMAGE! The snake is OBLITERATED! Moor immediately casts Cure Wounds from the Staff of Healing: "I got him." Aramil restored to 15 HP.

03
Baboon Swarm
Running Fight • ~6 Turns • Friendly Fire Incident
▼

Moor opens defensively with Shield of Faith on Lavender. Aramil's Eldritch Blast (11 to hit) connects for 9 damage — one baboon down! Lavender fires her own Eldritch Blast and rolls a NATURAL 1 — the beam goes wide and hits MOOR for 5 friendly fire damage!

Baboons bite Lavender for 5 damage. She retaliates with Hellish Rebuke — 10 fire damage that spreads to 2 more baboons, killing them both!

Bob makes the play of the encounter — screams down the hallway drawing the entire swarm, then Spider Climbs onto the ceiling with Stealth 27. Baboons charge past him into the spike trap room. Genius.

04
The Final Escape
Skill Challenge • Real-Time Timer • Rival Ambush • T-Rex Fireball
▼

Bob has the idol, the T-Rex just got hit by FIREBALL from the rival team, and it's JURASSIC PARK out here! Bob takes 9 fire damage (half on Dex save 24 — Evasion-lite).

The skill challenge is INTENSE. Two failures already — 13 damage each from scythe traps. One more and someone dies. Bob launches 36 feet with Boots of Striding and Springing — CLEAR!

Two rival mages Dimension Door in front of the exit. Moor casts Command: "FLEE!" — first mage saves. Aramil casts Phantasmal Force (conjuring raptors) — second mage rolls NAT 1 on Intelligence save! He panics, grabs his buddy, and they BOTH run!

Opal flips them off as the party sprints into Fizzleblast's safety bubble with seconds to spare. The first full party to ever survive.

Moor
Dragonborn Cleric
Sitting on a log, cleaning gore off his mace. Looks like a parent who survived a toddler's birthday party.
▼
Q: On the role of protector?
"Necessity. Someone has to carry the shield. They are squishy. I am scales. Simple math."
Q: Healing frustrations?
"Aramil talks. I heal. Opal smashes. I heal. Bob... Bob is Bob. I heal."
Q: Any regrets?
"Aramil found a shiny thing. I hope the shiny thing does not bite."
[Moor is found sitting heavily on a log near the beached canoes. He has removed his helmet and is methodically cleaning a mixture of gorilla gore and snake slime off his mace with a rag. He looks like a parent who has just survived a toddlers' birthday party at a trampoline park. He does not look up as Elswyth approaches.]

Elswyth Pattle: Master Moor? Thank you for sitting with me. That was... quite the extraction. How are you feeling?

[Moor stops scrubbing. He stares at a dent in his shield. He exhales a puff of smoke from his nostrils.]

Moor: Tired. Sticky.

[He resumes scrubbing, harder.]

Elswyth Pattle: You seemed to take on the role of protector for the group. Is that a calling of Tyr, or just your nature?

[He pauses, looking over his shoulder at where Opal is loudly recounting a story to a tree.]

Moor: Necessity. Someone has to stand in front. Someone has to carry the shield. If I don't, they die. They are squishy. I am scales. It is simple math.

Elswyth Pattle: Let's talk about the Temple. The "Man and Crocodile" puzzle. You and Lavender had to coordinate perfectly to cross those pressure plates. What was going through your mind?

[Moor shifts. He looks uncomfortable. He taps the rag against his knee.]

Moor: She is... light. Very light. I was worried the plate would not click. Then I was worried she would step too fast. It was a trust exercise. I do not like trust exercises. I prefer hammers. Hammers are predictable. Tieflings with genies are not.

Elswyth Pattle: You had a significant moment with the undead — the skeletons in the gas room. You turned almost all of them to dust instantly.

[A rare, toothy grin flickers across his face, then vanishes.]

Moor: Tyr was watching. The scales must be balanced. Undead are heavy on the wrong side. I simply... removed the weight. It was efficient. I like efficiency.

Elswyth Pattle: Speaking of efficiency, you healed your companions repeatedly. Aramil went down, Opal was blind... is it frustrating?

[He sighs, a low, rumbling growl deep in his chest.]

Moor: It is the job. Aramil talks. I heal. Opal smashes. I heal. Bob... Bob is Bob. I heal. If I stop, the talking and smashing stops. Then it is just quiet. I like quiet. But I do not like dead friends. So, I heal.

Elswyth Pattle: Do you have any regrets about how the expedition went?

[Moor looks at the sky, which is notably, terrifyingly dark for this time of day. He narrows his eyes.]

Moor: We ran too much. We left loot. But... we are breathing. Regret is for the dead.

Elswyth Pattle: And what about the artifact? Aramil seems to have secured... something.

[Moor stops cleaning his mace. He looks toward Aramil's tent with a heavy, unblinking stare.]

Moor: Aramil found a shiny thing. He is happy. I hope the shiny thing does not bite.

[Moor stands up, his joints popping audibly. He places the clean mace back in its loop. He pats Elswyth gently on the head with a massive hand, almost knocking her over, and walks away toward the fire without another word.]

Lavender: It's a mood. Very gothic chic. I'm sure it's fine. Aramil did something nerdy, Bob did something sneaky, and now it's dark. We'll fix it after a nap. Or we won't. I have darkvision.

"Correlation does not imply causation! Perhaps it is a very long, sudden, magical eclipse?"
Q: Show Fizzleblast?
"Show him? And let him take the credit? No. This is MY discovery."
[Aramil is crouched in the shadows behind the supply crates, frantically scribbling in a leather-bound journal. He looks gaunt, his fedora is stained with sweat and soot, and he keeps checking over his shoulder. He clutches a heavy satchel to his chest. He looks like a man vibrating with caffeine and paranoia.]

Elswyth Pattle: Professor? Or is it Mr. Aramil?

[Aramil jumps, snapping the book shut. He adjusts his glasses crookedly.]

Aramil: Adjunct Professor Emeritus! Or... just Aramil is fine for the paperwork. Just don't put "Assistant." My father reads these, you know.

Elswyth Pattle: Right. You seemed very focused on the murals and the history of the temple. Did you find what you were looking for?

Aramil: [He lowers his voice, leaning in.] I found vindication, Elswyth. This isn't just a "temple." It's a complex solar-locking mechanism! The Man and the Crocodile? It's metaphor! Symbology! The others, they just see loot. I see history. I see tenure.

Elswyth Pattle: You tried to speak to the giant constrictor snake. You called it "Brother." That was... a bold strategy.

[He grimaces, rubbing his ribs where the snake squeezed him.]

Aramil: It was a diplomatic attempt! I speak with animals! Usually they are more conversational. "Where is the nut?" "I saw a hawk." This one was... rude. Philistine. It didn't appreciate the cultural significance of not eating me. But I survived! I survived to document it.

Elswyth Pattle: Let's talk about the T-Rex. You went for the idol.

[Aramil clutches his bag tighter. His knuckles are white.]

Aramil: The idol belongs in a museum! Or... at the very least, in my study at the Royal Community College. It was hanging there. The T-Rex was a variable I calculated. I used Phantasmal Force. I distracted the rival party! It was brilliant!

Elswyth Pattle: Aramil... the sun is gone. The entire jungle is in darkness. Do you think that has anything to do with the artifact you took?

[Aramil freezes. He looks at the bag. He looks at the sky. He looks at Elswyth. He starts sweating profusely.]

Aramil: Correlation does not imply causation! That is basic science! Perhaps it is... an eclipse? A very long, sudden, magical eclipse? Look, I secured the asset. The asset is safe. If the sun has decided to take a sabbatical, that is hardly my fault. Dammit Elswyth, I'm an archeologist, not a meteorologist!

Elswyth Pattle: Are you going to show Fizzleblast what you found?

[He laughs, a high-pitched, hysterical sound.]

Aramil: Ha! Show him? And let him take the credit? No, no, no. This is my discovery. Aramil's Discovery. Has a ring to it, doesn't it?

[Aramil stands up abruptly, shoving the journal into his pocket. He looks wildly around the camp, pulls his fedora down low over his eyes, and scurries away toward the latrines, clutching his satchel like it contains his own heart.]
